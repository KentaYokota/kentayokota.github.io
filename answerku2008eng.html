京都大学入試英作文解答2008年度

Ⅲ

(1)
子供のころに、本を読んで感動したり、わくわくしたりした思い出は、一生消えることのないほど強烈なものである。子供は未知の世界に対して新鮮な好奇心を持ち、想像力が豊かであるため、本の世界のなかで生きることができるのだ。成長して様々な試練に出会ったときに、そのような経験が思わぬ力を発揮する場合がある。

解答例1

The memories of being moved or excited while reading a book during childhood are so powerful that they never disappear. Children have a fresh curiosity towards the unknown world and have rich imaginations, which is why they can live within the world of books. When they grow up and encounter various challenges, such experiences can unexpectedly demonstrate their power.

解答例2

The memories of being moved or excited while reading a book during childhood are so powerful that they never disappear. Children have a fresh curiosity towards the unknown world and have rich imaginations, which is why they can live within the world of books. When they grow up and encounter various challenges, such experiences can unexpectedly demonstrate their power.

解答例3

The recollections of being touched or excited while reading a book in childhood are powerful enough to never fade. Children possess a fresh curiosity regarding the unknown realms of the world and have a wealth of imagination, allowing them to inhabit the world of books. When they grow and face various trials, such experiences can unexpectedly exhibit their strength.

解答例4

The memories of being moved or excited while reading a book during childhood are incredibly powerful and everlasting. Children harbor a newfound curiosity towards the unfamiliar world and possess rich imaginations, which allow them to inhabit the world of books. When they mature and navigate through various obstacles, such experiences can unexpectedly showcase their potency.

解答例5

Children have a fresh curiosity about the unknown world and vivid imaginations, enabling them to live within the world of books. As they grow and face various challenges, such experiences can unexpectedly demonstrate their power.

解答例6

Children possess a fresh curiosity towards the unknown world and rich imaginations, making it possible for them to live within the world of books. When they grow up and face various trials, such experiences can unexpectedly demonstrate their strength.

(2)
コンクリートの建物に囲まれ、機能第一主義の無機質な都会で生活していると、鳥や虫の鳴き声に耳をすませたり、名も知らぬような草木に目をやったりしながら、季節の微妙な移り変わりを実感するようなことがめっきり少なくなってきたように思う。もっと心に余裕を持ち、一回きりのかけがいのない人生をうるおいのあるものにしたいと考えて、田舎に移住することを決断する人が最近増えてきているのも無理からぬことである。

解答例1

Living in a concrete jungle surrounded by functionalist architecture, it seems that the opportunity to appreciate the subtle changes of the seasons by listening to the chirping of birds or insects and looking at unfamiliar plants has become increasingly rare. It's understandable that more people are deciding to move to the countryside to gain more mental space and make their one-time-only precious life more fulfilling.

解答例2

It appears that urban life, encompassed by concrete buildings and functionalist design, has caused a decrease in the ability to feel the subtle changes of the seasons by listening to the sounds of birds or insects and admiring unfamiliar plants. Due to this, it's no surprise that more individuals are deciding to move to rural areas, hoping to have more mental peace and to create a more enriched, one-time-only life.

解答例3

Living in a sterile, inorganic city surrounded by concrete structures, it seems that the experiences of listening to birds chirping or insects buzzing and observing unfamiliar flora, which evoke the subtle changes of the seasons, have become increasingly rare. It's no wonder that individuals are increasingly deciding to move to the countryside, with the desire to have more emotional space and to create a more fulfilling, one-time-only life.

解答例4

It feels like the ability to appreciate the subtle changes of the seasons by listening to the chirping of birds or insects and observing unfamiliar plants has become increasingly scarce, living in a function-first, inorganic city surrounded by concrete buildings. As a result of this, it's not surprising that more people are deciding to move to rural areas, hoping to have more mental space and to make their one-time-only life more enriching.

解答例5

In a city of concrete buildings and functionalistic design, it seems that the ability to experience the subtle changes of the seasons by listening to the chirping of birds or insects and observing unfamiliar plants has decreased significantly. It's no wonder that more individuals have recently made the decision to move to the countryside, hoping to have more mental space and to create a more enriched, one-time-only life.
